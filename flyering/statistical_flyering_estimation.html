To accurately calculate $Q_L$, we must estimate its components using field data. This section explores how we model flyer decay (Survival Analysis) using maximum likelihood estimation, and how we apply Bayesian Priors based on physical location attributes like amenity type and dwell times.

4. Deep Research Prompts

The final deliverable requires a prototype outputting a Heatmap of Expected Conversion using a Bayesian Hierarchical Model. The following research vectors are required to finalize the statistical architecture.

1

Survival Modeling

Research "non-parametric survival analysis for intermittent observations." Methodology required to estimate object lifespan based solely on sporadic photographic checks.

2

Point Process Modeling

Explore "Spatial Poisson Point Processes." Determine feasibility of modeling flyer population as a point process realization conditioned on Cambridge traffic and municipal legal restrictions.

3

Multi-Armed Bandit (MAB)

Formalize flyering as an Exploration vs. Exploitation algorithm. Define the threshold for placing in a proven spot vs. querying a new OpenStreetMap candidate board.

4

Exogenous Correlation

Quantify the correlation between "Amenity Type" (cafe vs. laundry) and "Engagement Latency." Validate hypothesis regarding wait times and board engagement duration.
