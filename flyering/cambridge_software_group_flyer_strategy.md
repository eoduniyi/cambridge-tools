# Cambridge Software Developer Group: Flyering Strategy Summary

This document outlines an optimal strategy for placing flyers to promote the Cambridge Software Developer Group, which meets at the Cambridge Public Library - Main Branch on Broadway. 

The target demographic consists of degree-holding professionals looking to upskill, relearn basics, or engage with a technical social community.

## ⚖️ Legal Guidelines & Regulations

Cambridge strictly enforces ordinances regarding postering to prevent "visual clutter."

1.  **Public Infrastructure is Prohibited:** Do not affix flyers to utility poles, light posts, traffic signs, city trees, or hydrants. Fines can reach **$300 per violation**.
2.  **Private Property:** Always obtain explicit permission from the business manager or owner before posting.
3.  **Library Protocol:** For library boards, flyers must be submitted to staff for approval and hanging. Do not pin them yourself.
4.  **University Property:** Harvard and MIT have private policies; generally, only university-affiliated groups can post on their kiosks.

## 💡 Effective Flyering Pro-Tips

* **The Scanner Strategy:** Include a large, high-contrast QR code in the corner. Professionals are often in a rush; "scan and run" is more common than reading.
* **Color Palette:** Use bright or neon paper for grocery store boards to stand out among the clutter.
* **Supplies:** Carry blue painter's tape (for glass/walls) and a stapler/push-pins (for cork boards). 
* **Timing:** Check your flyers every 2 weeks. Busy boards are often cleared on Monday mornings by staff.
